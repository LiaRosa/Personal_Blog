    Fragments of Thoughts that Once Were


Feb 1, 2026


Walked with a friend to buy groceries, we both stood under the beams of the moon. I mentioned my favorite story, one that my father used to tell me:

 A man who tended his land all day,

Never resting, never resting, always busy —

The Moon approached him one night, begged him to go home,

But there can be no home without labour. 

As punishment, for ignoring her pleas,

  he was made to tend his land eternally, 

His wife and child, figments of the past.


 She lent me her ear, eyes searching for the farmer and his ox. Is he still tending to his land? I shrugged, my fingers numb from the cold, I like to think the Moon granted him peace eventually, she is after all a guardian. We stood in silence, no movement from either of us; Our breaths were almost still that we could have passed as ghosts. I smiled brightly at her, holding out my hand. It’s cold, let’s go inside. We ran toward the dim light of the store, laughing, our forms flickering like two stars in the sky.

Feb 1, 2026


Shadows are essentially a proof of existence.  Even while not looking directly at the tree, its shadow must mean that it exists; it moves, as if it were its own separate entity. It would be interesting to think of when there are no shadows. When light hits an object or a living being in a certain way, no shadow is produced. Would it really exist? If we could not see it, and there was no shadow, can we say something is real?

Mar 30, 2026

Lines Written on a Rainy Walk


A forgotten roll lays on the ground,

Maybe someone was hungry, 

And that hunger must have fled

And taken flight

Because now the roll is covered in soil,

The rain, dusting it over.


I am not the only one

Who passes by the roll,

Eyes glazing over, lips pursed,

Littering—

I however, feel as the roll


Feel the people passing by me, 

Watching, observing, but never stopping

Remaining untouched,

I am untouched,

Untouched –


In the end

The birds and the embrace of a mother

Will cradle my broken begotten body,

Will love only come at death?

Does one have to die,

To feel the warmth?

To a Friend,


It’s almost the end of the semester. Today, the weather is beautiful, the birds are singing again, and the daffodil bulbs look about ready to bloom. I hope that you can feel the beginning of spring, wherever you are now. I went back home…the same place we both called home — did you think of home? Of the warmth that comes when stepping foot in a city that has seen you grow — I went to the mall, the one that we both talked about going during high school, teenagers were everywhere, a reminder of what we once were. The spot that we both call “ our favorite”, still stands. I look away. My throat dry, a huge lump of possibilities being pushed down, hand clenching shirt, clawing at my heart, tear it out, tearing it out.

Eyes burning, heat flaring behind my skull, get out, leave now.

I wish you could have gone back home, you should have been here, standing where I am.

I’m sorry — I hope you found peace.

April 6, 2026


Humanity shifted the moment we decided to hunt whales. 

Whales, who are one of our eldest ancestors, whose eyes have knowledge 

that we can only dream of learning. 

Whose songs, up close, pull deep, deep inside you,

Who stay with their dead calfs, pushing them for days,

 Mourning —

 When the last Dodo was killed for being kind, 

we doomed our planet. 

When the last Stellar’s cow whose only fault was her own curiosity, 

approaching nearby boats, then,

we must have cursed our future generations. 

When sharks were finned for sport, their hearts hanging outside their jaws, 

fear and desperation the last emotion they felt. 

Their corpse on display —

When the great lonesome tortoise, George, 

who saw the beginning of civilization, had died, we died with him.

When pigeons were taken as instruments in war; 

messengers that took flight, awarded for heroics, 

only to be called pests, did we fail then?

Did we fail when we sent a stray into space, 

no return flight —

Did we advance as a society or did we fail one another? 

Did our mother,    the earth,    weep when she heard,

“We will drill, baby, drill”; 

when her children d e p a r t e d this world, when her lands were burned. 

We go to the stars above, searching for life, searching for meaning, 

While the ground beneath our feet dries up, water acidic,

organisms that have existed since the time of creation, 

now only 1,000, 

                    now only 100, 

                                        now only 10, 

                                                      soon none.


When the whales were hunted, did they cry, 

tears filling up the seas, 

their calves purposely used as bait. 

Did they decide to stay alongside their only child, 

knowing, 

knowing,

knowing 

that their end would come.
