April Showers

Author

Marlin Alejandro

On this page

  • April

The saying goes that April showers brings May flowers. The penultimate month for me; four years really pass by.

I initially entered my spring semester with a feeling of fulfillment but also dread. Dread for what comes next, for what might be lost, dread of closing this chapter of my life. I still remember hearing my older sister talk about Wellesley College. I was captivated by this small liberal arts college and I made it my dream to apply there. Two years of convincing and proving that I can be independent to my parents, two years of meticulously planning the financial situations that will come, calculating the aid and coverage. Two years of crying over arguments with my parents as we discussed the idea of me leaving. No one has ever studied out-of-state; not in my family, or anyone we knew. In our culture, that is not something that exists. You live with your family, until you marry, and sometimes, even married you still live with them—and how could I choose to leave. Because that is what I was doing. I was being selfish in choosing to leave, knowing my sisters didn’t have the opportunity to, knowing a million family concerns would not be on my shoulders, knowing that I was leaving a heavier burden on my family.

                   "Show me the heart unfettered by foolish dreams, and I'll show you a happy man."
       
  "But only in their dreams can men truly be free"

But, I wanted to be free in my dreams of Wellesley. I had already envisioned myself in her embrace, how could I refuse this call? I feared I would resent my family if I didn’t go. So I left—

I have come to learn that Wellesley while my dream, like any dream goes, can be good or bad. Nightmares are dreams too, and in all the love I have for Wellesley, there were times where I wished I was home, where I felt confined in the four walls we call dorm and wondered if I truly belonged. Even with my friends, I felt lonely at times. Humans at their core, desire and need human connection. I was making connections at Wellesley, but the connections I craved the most were at home, in New York. But my life at Wellesley, like all things—passes.

April

This April has been what I call my month of contemplation. My month of magical thinking. Maybe it was because I was reaching my end (college end that is), and after four years I have learned the in’s and out’s of Wellesley; her secrets and my fears and dreams. In her embrace I learned to deconstruct myself and accept the jagged pieces. ‘Identity’, that was what my pediatric psychiatrist had said I struggled with. The struggles of 12 year old me continue to haunt me. It sounds more daunting and horrifying than what it was, or maybe it was that horrifying; the process itself was painful, I tore at myself but also stitched myself anew—and how wonderful it was afterwards. Like my skin was too tight for who I was, and now under the new stitches I had made, I could finally breathe. But with this newer skin of mine, I still was met with hurdles.

My head hurt everyday for the past two years, I thought myself the woman in The Yellow Wallpaper, and thought I would go mad, images of my head tearing open like the birth of Athena from Zeus’s head. And just like the Zeus, I too birthed something from my mind: a gentler version of myself. With pain, with my pain, I learned to care for myself in ways I couldn’t bother to before. Who knew pain could create such a thing.

This new version of myself was gentler, but not to others, only to me. She was made for me, and that was all the more special. She visited me in the quietest nights, when I would massage at my frail body, praying that whatever I did in the past to deserve this pain, was worth it. I figured that maybe I committed treason against a monarch—or I caused a war. I must have been very evil and cruel to be suffering. This was the only thing I could think of, otherwise, I would be distraught on the implications that my pain has no reason behind it.

Maybe it is all just a conjuration of the mind, any doctor visit further cemented the idea. I must have made up everything, but for what? I’m still trying to figure that out. But, even with all this pain and suffering that Wellesley has seen me experience/caused, she has also nurtured the wild animal in me that bites. A dog that bites is not a good dog, but why does a dog have to be good? Under whose eyes? God? Human? In her nature, I found peace and fulfillment, I truly think I was meant to lie on the ground and be overtaken by the moss. In her lustrous greens, I found joy and my bones always felt lighter.

Besides being my month of contemplation, it was also my month of isolation. Illness, besides my frequent migraines racked my body continuously, and I became even more familiar with the shadows of my room. I have become so familiar with the way the sun rose and shined on my bookshelf that I could tell what time it was simply by the way the beams of sun hit the wooden frame. I wanted to go home, being sick and alone is most depressing. I would look at my calendar—only a few more weeks—I’ll be home soon.

I have decided to forgo any negative thoughts and instead share the Wellesley that I love with my friends. I went on multiple car trips with no permanent destination in mind, only talk and music.
